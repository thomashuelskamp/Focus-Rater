How sharpness is measured

The dense black bar of tick lines is located automatically by finding the image column or row band with the highest Laplacian variance. You then click the positions of two scale labels to set the exact pixel-to-unit mapping. Sharpness is measured by scanning along the bar and, at each position, counting how many individual tick lines are resolvable in a local window — and how prominent each one is. Where ticks are in focus they appear as distinct dark troughs; where blurred they merge into a smooth gradient and score zero. The score at each position is resolved tick count × mean prominence, mapped to the scale value at that pixel.

HIGH — individual ticks clearly separated MED — ticks visible but soft or partially merged LOW — ticks indistinct, blurred together
