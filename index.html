How sharpness is measured

The bar of small tick lines is located by finding the image column/row band with the highest Laplacian variance. You then click the positions of two scale labels to set the exact pixel-to-unit mapping. The bar is always treated as vertical. Sharpness is measured by scanning along the bar and, at each position, counting how many individual tick lines are resolvable in a local window — and how prominent each one is. Where ticks are in focus they appear as distinct dark troughs; where blurred they merge into a smooth gradient and score zero. The score at each position is resolved tick count × mean prominence, mapped to the scale value at that pixel. Peak focus reports the single highest-scoring position. In σ auto-threshold mode, zone boundaries are set automatically from the score distribution: HIGH ≥ μ+0.5σ, LOW < μ−0.5σ.

PEAK — highest sharpness position HIGH — individual ticks clearly separated MED — ticks visible but soft or partially merged LOW — ticks indistinct, blurred together
